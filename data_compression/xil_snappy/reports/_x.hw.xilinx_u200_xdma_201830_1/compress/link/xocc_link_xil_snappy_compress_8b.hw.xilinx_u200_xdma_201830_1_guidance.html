Kernel clocks (and system clocks for some platforms) are scalable; they can preserve functionality at the cost of performance by running at a lower frequency than requested. To be scalable, a clock must be driven by an MMCM where the control registers for the MMCM can be set by the runtime over AXI4-Lite.
